Joey Daniels

About Me

Hi, my name is Joey Daniels and I’m a Canadian 110m hurdler who has recently graduated from Princeton University in the Spring of 2020. In my athletic career, I have had the honour of producing a medal on the world stage, producing a silver in the 110mh at the 2015 IAAF World Youth Championships in Cali, Colombia. I’ve represented Canada on the World stage on three occasions, and I have the goal of representing my country at the Olympic games in 2021. At Princeton, I was a second team All-American (2019 NCAA Championships), and I am the Ivy League record holder for the 60mh (indoor, 7.77) and the 110mh (outdoor, 13.70). I was a 5-time Ivy League Champion, winning three (out of four) indoor titles and two (out of three) outdoor titles for the sprint hurdles, helping my team go undefeated at all seven of the Ivy League Championships we contested. I served as one of the team captains in my senior year. From nine years of hurdling at an elite level, I have the ability to share my expertise with hurdlers of all levels to rethink, refine and elevate their performance.

I look forward to emphasizing both sprinting speed and hurdle technique/efficiency to make you the best hurdler you can be.
